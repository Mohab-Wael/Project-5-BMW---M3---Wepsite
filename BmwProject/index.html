BMW's M3 model

BMW's journey with the legendary M Division began in 1972 when the company established BMW Motorsport GmbH to support its racing efforts. This division soon became responsible for some of the most iconic high-performance vehicles in automotive history. The first road car developed by BMW M was the BMW M1, launched in 1978, a mid-engined supercar that combined race-ready engineering with stunning design. Although it wasn’t a commercial success due to its limited production, it laid the foundation for a new era. In 1985, BMW introduced the M5 (E28), a powerful yet refined sports sedan, followed by the M3 (E30) in 1986, a car that would go on to become a motorsport legend. These early models defined BMW M’s philosophy: precision engineering, thrilling performance, and everyday usability.
